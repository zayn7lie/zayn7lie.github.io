Approx. Integral

Equaling space is not necessary

Dividing a function and approx it to separated rectangles, then just calculate the areas of rectangles:

$$ S (\int_a^b{f(x)}dx) = \lim_{n \rightarrow \infty} \sum_{i = 0}^{n - 1} \Delta x f(x_i) = \lim_{n \rightarrow \infty}{\sum_{i = 0}^{n - 1}\frac{b - a}{n}f(a + \frac{b - a}{n}i)} $$

EX-CAL-INT-01-01: Prove $\int_0^1{x^9} = \frac{1}{n}((\frac{1}{n})^9 + (\frac{2}{n})^9 + \ldots + (\frac{n}{n})^9)$

EX-CAL-INT-01-02: Prove $\ln(n + 1) < \sum_{i = 1}^{n}\frac{1}{i} < \ln{n} + 1$

  • Riemann integral: left, right, midpoint = $f((x_1 + x_2)/2)$
  • Trapezoidal = (left + right)/2
  • Simpson

$$ y = \frac{(x - x_1)(x - x_2)}{(x_0 - x_1)(x_0 - x_2)}y_0 + \frac{(x - x_2)(x - x_0)}{(x_1 - x_2)(x_1 - x_0)}y_1 + \frac{(x - x_0)(x - x_1)}{(x_2 - x_0)(x_2 - x_1)}y_2 $$ $$ if \ (-h, y_0) (0, y_1) (h, y_2) \Rightarrow \int_{-h}^{h}ydx = \frac{h}{3}(y_0 + 4y_1 + y_2) $$ $$ \Rightarrow S = \frac{h}{3}((y_0 + 4y_1 + y_2) + (y_2 + 4y_3 + y_4) + \ldots + (y_{n - 2} + 4y_{n - 1} + y_n)) (n \ is \ even) $$

  • Error Bond

$$ E = \begin{cases} \text{Riemann} \frac{K(b - a)^3}{24n^2} (K = |f’’_{max}(x)|) \\ \text{Trapezoidal} \frac{K(b - a)^3}{12n^2} (K = |f’’_{max}(x)|) \\ \text{Simpson} \frac{K(b - a)^5}{180n^4} (K = |f^{(4)}_{max}(x)|) \end{cases} $$

Trans between different variables

$$ \int_{C}^{g(x)}{f(t)}dt, \text{assume } F(t) = \int{f(t)}dt \Rightarrow \int_{C}^{g(x)}{f(t)}dt = F(g(x)) - F(C) $$ $$ \text{Taking derivative in both sides:} \frac{d}{dx}{\int_{C}^{g(x)}{f(t)}dt} = \frac{d}{dx}F(g(x)) - \frac{d}{dx}F(C) $$ $$ F’(x) = f(x), F(C) \text{ is constant} \Rightarrow F’(g(x)) \cdot g’(x) - 0 = f(g(x)) \cdot g’(x) $$ $$ \therefore \frac{d}{dx}{\int_{C}^{g(x)}{f(t)}dt} = f(g(x)) \cdot g’(x), \frac{d}{dx}{\int_{h(x)}^{g(x)}{f(t)}dt} = f(g(x)) \cdot g’(x) - f(h(x)) \cdot h’(x) $$

HOW: How to integral - Technique

Substitution: If the function combines with its derivative

Rules: $\frac{df}{dx} = \frac{df}{dh} \cdot \frac{dh}{dx}$

  • $\int{F’(g(x)) \cdot g’(x)}{dx}$

$$ u = g(x), du = g’(x) dx \Rightarrow \int{F’(u)}{du} = F(u) + C = F(g(x)) + C $$

Trigonometric function: $\sqrt{1 - x^2}$, $\sqrt{1 + x^2}$, $\sqrt{x^2 - 1}$, $\frac{1}{1 + x^2}$, $\frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - x^2}}$

  • $\int{\frac{1}{x(x^2 + 1)}}{dx}$

$$ u = \arctan{x}, du = \frac{1}{x^2 + 1}dx, x = \tan{u} \Rightarrow \int{\frac{1}{\tan{u}}}{du} $$ $$ v = \sin{u}, dv = \cos{u}du \Rightarrow \int{\frac{1}{v}}{dv} = \ln{v} + C = \ln{\sin{u}} + C = \ln{\sin{\arctan{x}} + C} $$

  • $\int{\sec^4{x} \tan^3{x}}{dx}$

$$ \tan{\theta} = \sec^2{\theta} - 1 = \int\sec^2{\theta}d\theta $$

then Integral:

  • $\int{\frac{c}{(ax + b)^n}}dx$

$$ \int{\frac{c}{(ax + b)^n}}dx = \frac{c}{a} \cdot \frac{1}{(1 - n)(ax+b)^{n-1}} + C \ or \ = \frac{c}{a} \cdot \ln{|ax + b|} + C \ when \ n = 1 $$

  • $\int{\frac{gx + h}{(ax^2 + bx + c)^n}}dx$

$$ \int{\frac{gx + h}{(ax^2 + bx + c)^n}}dx = \int{\frac{gx + h}{(a(x + \frac{b}{2a})^2 + c - \frac{b^2}{4a})^n}}dx, u = x + \frac{b}{2a} \Rightarrow \int{\frac{iu + j}{(au^2 + k^2)^n}}du $$ $$ (1) $$ $$ \int{\frac{iu}{(au^2+k^2)^n}}du, v = au^2 + k^2, \frac{1}{2a}dv = udu $$ $$ \Rightarrow \frac{i}{2a}\int{\frac{1}{v^n}}dv \Rightarrow \frac{i}{2a} \cdot \frac{1}{(1 - n)(au^2 + k^2)^{n - 1}} + C \ or \ \frac{i}{2a}\ln{|au^2 + k^2|} + C \ when \ n = 1 $$ $$ (2) $$ $$ \ n = 1: v = \frac{u\sqrt{a}}{k} \Rightarrow \frac{j}{\sqrt{a}}\int{\frac{1}{v^2 + 1}dv} = \frac{j}{\sqrt{a}}\arctan{v} + C $$ $$ *n \neq 1: $$ $$ \int{\frac{1}{(x^2 + 1)^n}}dx, x = \tan{u}, dx = \sec^2{u}du \Rightarrow \int{\frac{1}{\sec^{2n - 2}{u}}}du = \int{\frac{\sec^2{u} - \tan^2{u}}{\sec^{2n - 2}{u}}}du $$ $$ = \int{\frac{\sec^2{u}}{\sec^{2n - 2}{u}}}du - \int{\frac{\tan{u}\sec{u}\tan{u}}{\sec^{2n - 1}{u}}}du, i’ = \frac{\tan{u}\sec{u}}{\sec^{2n - 1}{u}}, i = \frac{-1}{(2n - 2)\sec^{2n - 2}u} $$ $$ \Rightarrow \frac{x}{(2n - 2)(x^2 + 1)^{n - 1}} + \int{\frac{\sec^2{u}}{\sec^{2n - 2}{u}}}du - \int{\frac{sec^2{u}}{(2n - 2)\sec^{2n - 2}{u}}} $$ $$ = \frac{x}{(2n - 2)(x^2 + 1)^{n - 1}} + \frac{2n - 3}{2n - 2}\int{\frac{1}{(x^2 + 1)^{n - 1}}} $$ $$ \int\frac{1}{(ax^2 + b)^n} = \frac{2n - 3}{2b(n - 1)}\int\frac{1}{(ax^2 + b)^{n - 1}}dx + \frac{x}{2b(n - 1)(ax^2 + b)^{n - 1}} $$

HOW: How to apply integral to reality

Rate - Differential Equations

$$ \text{Informal:} \frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{x}{y} \Rightarrow ydy = xdx \Rightarrow \int{y}dy = \int{x}dx $$ $$ \text{*Formal Solution:} \frac{d}{dx}{y(t(x))} = \frac{dy}{dt} \cdot \frac{dt}{dx} \Leftrightarrow \int{(y(t(x)) \frac{dt}{dx})}dx = \int{(y \frac{dt}{dx})}dx = \frac{y^2}{2} $$

  • Carrying Capacity:

$$ \frac{dN}{dt} = rN(1 - \frac{N}{K}) $$ $$ \Rightarrow \frac{1}{N(K - N)} \frac{dN}{dt} = \frac{1}{K} (\frac{1}{N} + \frac{1}{K - N}) \frac{dN}{dt} = \frac{r}{K} \Rightarrow \int{(\frac{1}{N} + \frac{1}{K - N})\frac{dN}{dt}}dt = \int{r}dt $$ $$ \ln{N} - \ln{(K - N)} = rt + C_1 \Rightarrow N = \frac{K}{1 + e^{-rt}} $$

$N$ is the population size, $r$ is the intrinsic growth rate, $K$ is the carrying capacity of the local environment, and $dN/dt$, the derivative of $N$ with respect to time $t$, is the rate of change in population with time.

$$ x\frac{d^2y}{dx^2} + 2\frac{dy}{dx} = 0 \Rightarrow \int{(x\frac{d^2y}{dx} + \frac{dy}{dx})}dx = \int{-\frac{dy}{dx}}dx \Rightarrow x\frac{dy}{dx} = -y $$

HOW: How to apply in Polar Coordinate

WHY: Why we should learn about Polar Coordinate - Cycloid & Parametric Curves

$$ \begin{cases} x = r(\theta - \sin\theta) \\ y = r(1 - \cos\theta) \end{cases} $$

$$ \frac{dy}{d\theta} = \frac{dy}{dx}\frac{dx}{d\theta} \Rightarrow \frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{dy/d\theta}{dx/d\theta}, \frac{d^2y}{dx^2} = \frac{d}{dx}(\frac{dy}{dx}) = \frac{\frac{d}{d\theta}(\frac{dy}{dx})}{\frac{dx}{d\theta}} $$

Why $d^2y/dx^2 \neq \frac{d^2y/d\theta^2}{d^2x/d\theta^2}?$

If you remember “$y$ and $x$ are not in the same manner” that I mentioned before, you would find that in this equation, the left is differentiation respecting to $x$, or they are in different stage, but the right side turns the $x$ and $y$ into same stage.

$$ L = \int_{a}^{b}{\sqrt{1 + \frac{dy^2}{dx^2}}}dx = \int_{\alpha}^{\beta}{\sqrt{1 + (\frac{dy/dt}{dx/dt})^2}}\frac{dx}{dt}dt = \int_{\alpha}^{\beta}{\sqrt{\frac{dx^2}{dt^2} + \frac{dy^2}{dt^2}}}dt $$

WHAT: What is Polar Coordinates

$$ A(x, y) \Rightarrow A(r\cos{\theta}, r\sin{\theta}) \Rightarrow A(r, \theta) $$
