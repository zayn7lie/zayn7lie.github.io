WHAT: What is differentiation - Partial Derivatives

WHY: Limit & Continuity - Differentiable

  • Limit

    $$ \lim_{(x, y) \rarr (a, b)}f(x, y) = L $$

    When $f \rarr L$ no matter what direction $(x, y) \rarr (a, b)$, the limit of $f$ in $(a, b)$ exists.

    If $f(x, y) \rarr L_1$ as $(x, y) \rarr (a, b)$ along a path $C_1$ and $f(x, y) \rarr L_2$ as $(x, y) \rarr (a, b)$ along a path $C_2$, where $L_1 \neq L_2$, then $\lim_{(x, y) \rarr (a, b)}f(x, y)$ does not exist.

    • Limit along axis: let $y = 0$ or $x = 0$
    • Limit along specific line: $y = x$

  • Continuity: $\lim_{(x, y) \rarr (a, b)}f(x, y) = f(a, b)$

WHAT: What is Partial Derivatives

  • Algebra: Definition $$ \frac{\partial}{\partial x}f = f_x(a, b) = \lim_{h \rarr 0}\frac{f(a + h, b) - f(a, b)}{h} = g’(a) \ (g(x) = g(x, b)) = \lim_{h \rarr 0}\frac{g(a + h) - g(a)}{h} $$ $$ \frac{\partial}{\partial y}f = f_y(a, b) = \lim_{h \rarr 0}\frac{f(a, b + h) - f(a, b)}{h} = g’(b) \ (g(y) = g(a, y)) = \lim_{h \rarr 0}\frac{g(b + h) - g(b)}{h} $$

    • Differentiable: $\Delta{z} = f_x(a, b)\Delta{x} + f_y(a, b)\Delta{y} + \epsilon_1\Delta{x} + \epsilon_2\Delta{y}, (\epsilon_1, \epsilon_2) \rarr (0, 0) \rArr (\Delta{x}, \Delta{y}) \rarr (0, 0)$
    • Differentials: $dz = \frac{\partial z}{\partial x} dx + \frac{\partial z}{\partial y} dy$

  • Geometry: Tangent Planes $$ A(x - x_0) + B(y - y_0) + C(z - z_0) = 0 \rArr z - z_0 = f_x(x_0, y_0)(x - x_0) + f_y(x_0, y_0)(y - y_0) $$

    • Linear Approximations

HOW: How to do Partial Derivatives

  • Chain Rule $$ (1) \frac{dz}{dt} = \frac{\partial f}{\partial x} \frac{dx}{dt} + \frac{\partial f}{\partial y} \frac{dy}{dt} $$ $$ (2) \frac{\partial z}{\partial s} = \frac{\partial z}{\partial x} \frac{\partial x}{\partial s} + \frac{\partial z}{\partial y} \frac{\partial y}{\partial s}, \frac{\partial z}{\partial s} = \frac{\partial z}{\partial x} \frac{\partial x}{\partial s} + \frac{\partial z}{\partial y} \frac{\partial y}{\partial s} $$ $$ (3) General: \frac{\partial u}{\partial t_i} = \frac{\partial u}{\partial x_1} \frac{\partial x_1}{\partial t_i} + \frac{\partial u}{\partial x_2} \frac{\partial x_2}{\partial t_i} + \frac{\partial u}{\partial x_3} \frac{\partial x_3}{\partial t_i} + \ldots $$

HOW: How to Apply to Vector

$$ D_u{f(x, y)} = \nabla{f(x, y)} \cdot \vec{u} $$

  • $D_u{f(x, y)} = \vec{P’Q’}$:\ - Gradient Vector: moving in a tangent plant with a specific direction
    • $\nabla{f(x, y)}$ represents tangent plant
    • $\vec{u}$ represents a direction (in unit), or $\vec{u} = <\hat{a}, \hat{b}>$

WHAT: What is integral - Multiple Integrals

WHAT: What is double integral

$$ \iint\limits_R{f(x, y)}dA = \lim_{max \Delta x_i, \Delta y_i \rarr 0}\sum_{i = 1}^{m}\sum_{j = 1}^{n}f(x_{ij}^, y_{ij}^) \Delta A_{i,j}(Double \ Riemann \ Sum, Midpoint: x^,y^ \rarr \bar{x}, \bar{y}) $$

HOW: How to calculate iterated integrals?

$$ \int_a^b\int_c^d{f(x, y)}dA = \int_a^b[\int_c^df(x, y)dy]dx = \int_c^d[\int_a^bf(x, y)dx]dy $$

HOW: How to apply to reality

Volume

Region of $x_1, x_2, y_1(x), y_2(x), Assume \ x_1 \leq x_2, y_1 \leq y_2$:

$$ V = \int_{y_1(x)}^{y_2(x)}\int_{x_1}^{x_2}f(x, y)dA = \int_{x_1}^{x_2}[\int_{y_1(x)}^{y_2(x)}f(x, y)dy]dx$$
