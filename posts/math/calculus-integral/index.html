Calculus - Integral

09/19/2023
4 minute read

The knowledge marked with * is not necessary in Calculus II

WHY: Why do we want to find out the integral

  • Find out the area
  • Find out some physics parameters

WHAT: What is integral

Riemann integral

Dividing a function and approx it to separated rectangles, then just calculate the areas of rectangles:

$$ S (\int_a^b{f(x)}dx) = \lim_{n \rarr \infty}{\sum_{i = 1}^{n}\frac{b - a}{n}f(a + \frac{b - a}{n}i)} $$

eg. $$ \int_0^1{x^9} = \frac{1}{n}((\frac{1}{n})^9 + (\frac{2}{n})^9 + \ldots + (\frac{n}{n})^9) $$

Trans between different variables

$$ \int_{C}^{g(x)}{f(t)}dt, assume \ F(t) = \int{f(t)}dt \rArr \int_{C}^{g(x)}{f(t)}dt = F(g(x)) - F(C) $$ $$ Taking \ derivative \ in \ both \ sides: \frac{d}{dx}{\int_{C}^{g(x)}{f(t)}dt} = \frac{d}{dx}F(g(x)) - \frac{d}{dx}F(C) $$ $$ F’(x) = f(x), F(C) \ is \ constant \rArr F’(g(x)) \cdot g’(x) - 0 = f(g(x)) \cdot g’(x) $$ $$ \therefore \frac{d}{dx}{\int_{C}^{g(x)}{f(t)}dt} = f(g(x)) \cdot g’(x), \frac{d}{dx}{\int_{h(x)}^{g(x)}{f(t)}dt} = f(g(x)) \cdot g’(x) - f(h(x)) \cdot h’(x) $$

HOW: How to integral - Technique

WHY: Why we use techniques to solve the integral problems?

  • The function cannot be antiderivatived directly.
  • The process of original function to integral is too challenging.
  • Direct integral cannot give us the answer.

WHAT: What is the techniques of integral

  • It could turn one function which cannot or is challenging to integral to a simple function
  • The tricks that we can use in the process of integral.

HOW: How to use the techniques to integral

If the function combines with its derivative: Substitution

Rules: $\frac{df}{dx} = \frac{df}{dh} \cdot \frac{dh}{dx}$

  • $\int{F’(g(x)) \cdot g’(x)}{dx}$

$$ u = g(x), du = g’(x) dx \rArr \int{F’(u)}{du} = F(u) + C = F(g(x)) + C $$

  • $\int{\tan{x}}{dx}$

$$ \int{\tan{x}}{dx} = \int{\frac{\sin{x}}{\cos{x}}}{dx} $$ $$ u = \cos{x}, du = -\sin{x} dx \rArr \int{\frac{-du}{u}} = - \ln{u} + c = -\ln{\cos{x}} + C $$

  • $\int{\frac{1}{x(x^2 + 1)}}{dx}$

$$ u = \arctan{x}, du = \frac{1}{x^2 + 1}dx, x = \tan{u} \rArr \int{\frac{1}{\tan{u}}}{du} $$ $$ v = \sin{u}, dv = \cos{u}du \rArr \int{\frac{1}{v}}{dv} = \ln{v} + C = \ln{\sin{u}} + C = \ln{\sin{\arctan{x}} + C} $$

  • $\int{\sec^4{x} \tan^3{x}}{dx}$ $$ \tan{\theta} = \sec^2{\theta} - 1 = \int\sec^2{\theta} $$

  • *$\int{\frac{1}{\tan^n{x}}}dx$ $$ \sec^2 - \tan^2 = 1 \rArr \int{\frac{\sec^2 - \tan^2}{\tan^n{x}}}dx = \int{\frac{\sec^2{x}}{\tan^n{x}}}dx - \int{\frac{1}{\tan^{n - 2}{x}}}dx $$ $$ \rArr \int{\frac{1}{u^n}}du - \int{\frac{\sec^2 - \tan^2}{\tan^{n - 2}{x}}}dx = \frac{1}{(1 - n)u^{n - 1}} - \int{\frac{\sec^2{x}}{\tan^{n - 2}{x}}}dx + \int{\frac{1}{\tan^{n - 4}{x}}}dx $$ $$ = \frac{1}{(1 - n)u^{n - 1}} - \frac{1}{(3 - n)u^{n - 3}} + \frac{1}{(5 - n)u^{n - 5}} - \frac{1}{(7 - n)u^{n - 7}} + \ldots (\pm \int{\frac{1}{\tan{x}}}dx) + C $$ $$ \rArr \int{\frac{1}{\tan{x}}}dx, v = \sin{x}, dv = \cos{x}dx \rArr \int{\frac{1}{v}}dv = \ln{|v|} + C $$

If the function combines with a simple function’s derivative: Int by part

  • Reduce Power: $\int{x \cdot \sin x}dx$
  • Eliminate complex function: $\int{\ln x}dx$

E.P. Find every function integral then int by part: $\int{\arcsin x \cdot \ln x}dx$

Other techniques (Not belongs to Integral)

Partial Fractions

$$ f(x) = \frac{A(x)}{B(x)} = f_1(x) + \frac{C(x)}{D(x)}, deg(A) >= deg(B) \ or \ deg(C) < deg(D) $$ $$ D(x) = (a_1x+b_1)^{p_1}…(a_ix+b_i)^{p_i}(u_1x^2 + v_1x + w_1)^{r_1}…(u_jx^2 + v_jx + w_j)^{r_j} $$ $$ \rArr \sum_{i, k}\frac{C_{i, k}}{(a_ix+b_i)^{p_k}} + \sum_{j, h}\frac{g_{j, h}x + h_{j, h}}{(u_jx^2 + v_jx + w_j)^{r_h}} $$

then Integral:

  • $\int{\frac{c}{(ax + b)^n}}dx$

$$ \int{\frac{c}{(ax + b)^n}}dx = \frac{c}{a} \cdot \frac{1}{(1 - n)(ax+b)^{n-1}} + C \ or \ = \frac{c}{a} \cdot \ln{|ax + b|} + C \ when \ n = 1 $$

  • $\int{\frac{gx + h}{(ax^2 + bx + c)^n}}dx$

$$ \int{\frac{gx + h}{(ax^2 + bx + c)^n}}dx = \int{\frac{gx + h}{(a(x + \frac{b}{2a})^2 + c - \frac{b^2}{4a})^n}}dx, u = x + \frac{b}{2a} \rArr \int{\frac{iu + j}{(au^2 + k^2)^n}}du $$ $$ (1) $$ $$ \int{\frac{iu}{(au^2+k^2)^n}}du, v = au^2 + k^2, \frac{1}{2a}dv = udu $$ $$ \rArr \frac{i}{2a}\int{\frac{1}{v^n}}dv \rArr \frac{i}{2a} \cdot \frac{1}{(1 - n)(au^2 + k^2)^{n - 1}} + C \ or \ \frac{i}{2a}\ln{|au^2 + k^2|} + C \ when \ n = 1 $$ $$ (2) $$ $$ when \ n = 1: v = \frac{u\sqrt{a}}{k} \rArr \frac{j}{\sqrt{a}}\int{\frac{1}{v^2 + 1}dv} = \frac{j}{\sqrt{a}}\arctan{v} + C $$ $$ *when \ n \neq 1: $$ $$ (a) $$ $$ \int{\frac{j}{(au^2+k^2)^n}}du = \frac{j}{k^{2n}}\int{\frac{1}{((\frac{u\sqrt{a}}{k})^2 + 1)^n}}du, \tan{v} = \frac{u\sqrt{a}}{k}, \sec^2{v}dv = \frac{\sqrt{a}}{k}du $$ $$ \rArr \frac{j}{\sqrt{a}}\int{\frac{1}{\sec^{2n-2}{v}}dv} \rArr \int{\frac{1 +\cos^{n-1}{2v}}{2}dv} = \ldots = Av + \frac{1}{2^r}\int{\cos^o{2^rv}dv}(o \ is \ odd) $$ $$ \int{\cos{2^rv}(1 - \sin^2{2^rv})^e}dv \ (e \ is \ even), w = \sin{2^rv}, dw = 2^r\cos{}dv \rArr \int\frac{(1 - w^2)^e}{2^r}dw $$ $$ (b) $$ $$ \int{\frac{1}{(ax^2 + b)^n}}dx, u = \frac{1}{(ax^2 + b)^n}, u’ = \frac{-n \cdot 2ax}{(ax^2 + b)^{n + 1}}, v’ = 1, v = x $$ $$ \rArr \frac{x}{(ax^2 + b)^n} - \int{\frac{-n \cdot 2ax^2}{(ax^2 + b)^{n + 1}}}dx $$

Approx to limit

For integral $\int_a^b{f(x)}dx$, when there are some points that are not defined, we use $t$ to approx. the limit of those points:

$$ \int_a^b{f(x)}dx = \lim_{t \rarr b}\int_a^t{f(x)}dx $$
