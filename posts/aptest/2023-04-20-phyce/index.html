Electricity and Magnetism

04/16/23
2 minute read

Electricity

Micro

Force: Coulomb’s Law

$$ |\vec{F_E}| = \frac{1}{4 \pi \epsilon_0} |\frac{q_1 q_2}{r^2}| = \vec{E} \cdot q $$

  • $q_1$ & $q_2$ (Charge): Positive (Glass + Silk) Negative (Plastic + Fur)
    • electrons: neg (e)
    • Protons: pos (e)
    • neutrons & atoms: neutral

Field (Reason of Force): Gauss’s Law

$$ \Phi_E(Flux) = \oint{\vec{E}}{d\vec{A}} = \frac{Q}{\epsilon_0} \ Q = \begin{cases} \int{\lambda}{dL} \ \int{\sigma}{dA} \ \int{\rho}{dV} \end{cases} $$

  • $\oint{\vec{E}}$ (Gaussian Surface): enclosing with sphere or cylinder
    • Sphere: $\frac{Q}{\epsilon_0 \cdot 4 \pi L^2}$, non-conducting (L < r): $\frac{Q L}{\epsilon_0 \cdot 4 \pi r^3}$
    • Cylinder (wire): $\frac{\lambda}{\epsilon_0 \cdot 2 \pi L}$
    • Plate: $\frac{\sigma}{\epsilon_0 \cdot 2}$

Potential (Energy of Force)

$$ W_{a → b} = -\Delta{U}, U = k \frac{q q_0}{r} \ V = \frac{U}{q} = k \frac{q}{r} = E \cdot r $$

  • Equipotential Surfaces

Macro

  • Kirchhoff’s Rules

    • Current rule: for every junction, exists $I = \sum{I_i}$

    • Voltage rule: for every loop, exists $0 = \sum{V_i}$

Conductors

  • Charge distribution: surface, $\propto$ curvature
    • Electric field: inside = 0, surface: vertical
    • Potential: inside: same

Capacitors

$$ C = \frac{(\kappa) \epsilon_0 A}{d} = \frac{Q}{\Delta{V}} $$ $Q$: only one plate; $\kappa$: vacuum = 1, others > 1

  • other shape: $C = \frac{Q}{E d}$

  • Relationship with time (RC Circuit)

$$ Charging: \ \epsilon - i R - \frac{q}{C} = 0, i = \frac{d}{dt}{q} \ q = C \epsilon(1 - e^{-\frac{t}{R C}}) \ \ Discharging: \ -\frac{q}{C} - i R = 0, i = \frac{d}{dt}{q}, q = Q_0 e^{-\frac{t}{RC}} $$ $\tau = R C$ when $t = \tau$, $q = (1 - \frac{1}{e})Q_f$

  • enclosed loops

Inductance
